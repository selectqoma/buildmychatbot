# BuildMyChatbot global marketing and sales strategy

Created: 2026-04-28

## Product position

BuildMyChatbot should be sold as a done-for-you, source-grounded customer service chatbot for companies that already have support docs but still answer the same questions every day.

Core promise:

- Live in about two weeks.
- Answers only from indexed docs, help centers, FAQs, product pages, and approved sources.
- Escalates safely when confidence is low.
- Works as a website widget, embedded assistant, Slack/email handoff, or support intake flow.
- EU-first hosting and handover of source code, prompts, retrieval settings, and deployment config.
- Fixed project quote, optional monthly care, no platform lock-in.

This is not best positioned as "another chatbot platform." The stronger wedge is: "We build the support agent around your docs and workflows, then you own it."

## Best-fit client profile

Prioritize companies with these signals:

- Public help center, docs site, developer docs, FAQ, or academy content.
- Repetitive setup, billing, account, integration, troubleshooting, returns, or policy questions.
- Support demand across multiple regions or languages.
- Existing support team, but not a giant enterprise with a mature AI procurement process.
- Clear pain from platform lock-in, compliance, privacy, or poor generic bot behavior.
- Support lives in tools like Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk, HubSpot, Help Scout, Jira Service Management, Slack, email, or custom CRMs.

Avoid at first:

- Tiny companies with no docs and low ticket volume.
- Very large enterprises where buying requires a long security/procurement cycle.
- Companies already deeply committed to Intercom Fin, Zendesk AI, Salesforce Agentforce, or a custom internal AI team unless the angle is a narrow proof-of-concept or EU/self-owned deployment.
- Regulated businesses that require full legal/security review before even a demo, unless there is a warm intro.

## Priority verticals

### 1. B2B SaaS with heavy product education

Why it fits: docs-heavy, repetitive setup questions, integrations, roles, billing, SSO, API limits, migration, and troubleshooting.

Strong pitch:

"Your help center already has the answers. We turn it into a branded support agent that cites sources and hands off cleanly when the answer needs a human."

- Head of Support
- Customer Success Lead
- COO

- Product Ops

Why it fits: account setup, verification, settlements, refunds, card issues, expense rules, accounting integrations, security, and compliance-sensitive support.

Best buyers:

- VP Customer Experience
- Head of Support Operations

- Product Support Manager

Why it fits: merchants ask repetitive questions about store setup, payments, domains, shipping, returns, apps, themes, marketplaces, and product catalog setup.

Strong pitch:

Best buyers:

- Merchant Support Lead
- CX Manager

Why it fits: fragmented users, urgent support, setup workflows, multilingual support, invoice/accounting questions, device/printer issues, and operational how-to questions.

Strong pitch:

"A support assistant for frontline users that can answer common workflow questions immediately and route urgent or account-specific issues to the right team."

Best buyers:

- Support Director

- Customer Success Lead

### 5. Healthcare, education, and compliance-heavy services

Why it fits: high need for careful routing, privacy-aware language, and source-grounded answers.

Strong pitch:

"A controlled assistant that answers from approved material and escalates anything sensitive, account-specific, or clinically/legally risky."

Best buyers:

- Patient/Student Support Lead
- Operations Director

## Message by buyer

### Head of Support / CX

Pain:

- Ticket volume is rising.
- Customers ignore docs.
- Generic bots create bad escalations.
- Support team spends time on repeat questions instead of complex issues.

Message:

"I can build a demo agent on your public help center that answers common support questions, cites the source, and escalates safely when unsure. You can test it before committing to anything."

### Founder / COO

Pain:

- Support is slowing growth.
- Hiring more agents is expensive.
- They do not want another SaaS subscription that owns the support workflow.

Message:

"You get a fixed-scope support agent in two weeks, deployed around your docs and workflows, with source code and deployment handed over."

### Product / Product Ops

Pain:

- Product questions reveal UX gaps but are buried in tickets.
- Docs are maintained but underused.
- Support insights are not making it back to roadmap.

Message:

"The assistant logs questions and unresolved topics, so product can see where users are confused before those issues become a larger support pattern."

### Compliance / Ops

Pain:

- AI support can hallucinate.
- Account-specific or regulated questions need careful escalation.
- Data hosting and ownership matter.

Message:

"The agent is source-constrained, logs confidence and sources, escalates by rule, and can be hosted in EU infrastructure or handed over to your team."

## Outreach strategy

### Offer

Lead with a free docs demo, not a call.

The offer:

"Send me your help center URL, or I can use your public docs. I will send back a small working demo with 10-15 realistic questions."

Why this works:

- Reduces buyer effort.
- Shows concrete quality.
- Avoids sounding like a generic AI pitch.
- Lets you personalize from public docs.
- Creates a natural follow-up: "Which answers were wrong or risky?"

### Outbound sequence

Day 1: Personalized email

- Mention 2-3 specific help center categories.
- Say why those topics are good chatbot candidates.
- Offer a free docs demo.
- Keep it under 120 words.

Day 3: Follow-up with sample questions

- Include 5 questions their users likely ask.
- Ask if they want a short demo answering those questions from their docs.

Day 7: Send a micro-demo or screenshot if possible

- Use public docs only.
- Show source citation and escalation.
- Ask for the right owner if they are not the buyer.

Day 14: Breakup email

- Mention one concrete repeated-support category.
- Ask whether support automation is owned by CX, product, or ops.

### Channels

Primary:

- Cold email to support/CX/customer success leaders.
- LinkedIn connection to Head of Support, CX, Product Ops, and COO.
- Founder-led posts showing "before and after" demos on public docs.

Secondary:

- Partner with small agencies building websites, help centers, and customer portals.
- Partner with Zendesk/Intercom/HubSpot implementation consultants who do not want to build custom AI.
- Target local SaaS communities in Belgium, Netherlands, France, UK, Nordics, Spain, LatAm, Australia, SEA, and MENA.

## Sales process

### Stage 1: Qualification

Ask:

- How many support tickets/conversations per month?
- What percentage are repetitive?
- Where are docs/help center/product FAQs?
- Which helpdesk/CRM do they use?
- What topics should never be answered automatically?
- What counts as a successful escalation?
- What languages matter?
- Any hosting/privacy constraints?

Disqualify if:

- No docs and no willingness to create approved content.
- Fewer than 200 support conversations/month unless the deal has strategic value.
- They want autonomous account actions before trust is established.
- They only want the cheapest chatbot widget.

### Stage 2: Demo

Build the demo around 10-15 realistic questions:

- 6 common questions the bot should answer.
- 3 ambiguous questions where it should ask a clarifying question.
- 3 risky/account-specific questions where it should escalate.
- 1 multilingual question if relevant.
- 1 "docs missing" question to show how gaps are logged.

### Stage 3: Paid project

Package the build as:

- Discovery and source audit.
- Content ingestion up to a defined page count.
- Retrieval and answer behavior tuning.
- Widget or handoff integration.
- Escalation rules.
- Analytics dashboard.
- Deployment.
- Handover docs.
- Two weeks support after launch.

### Stage 4: Monthly care

Offer optional care after the build:

- Monthly re-ingestion.
- Escalation review.
- Top unanswered questions report.
- Answer quality tuning.
- New topic onboarding.
- Help center gap report.

## Pricing guidance

The website currently says "fixed project quote." Keep that public positioning until there is enough sales data.

Suggested internal pricing bands:

- Starter build: EUR 3,500-6,000
  - One public help center, website widget, email handoff, basic analytics.
- Growth build: EUR 7,500-15,000
  - Multiple sources, Slack/helpdesk webhook, multilingual behavior, richer analytics, content gap review.
- Controlled deployment: EUR 15,000-35,000+
  - EU/private deployment, stricter security review, CRM/helpdesk integration, advanced routing, custom dashboard.
- Monthly care: EUR 750-3,000/month
  - Depends on conversation volume, re-ingestion frequency, tuning, and support SLA.

Do not lead with hourly pricing. Buyers are paying for reduced support load, faster response, and controlled AI deployment.

## 30-day execution plan

Week 1:

- Pick 30 Tier 1 prospects.
- Build a spreadsheet with company, region, vertical, help center URL, support tool if visible, likely buyer, LinkedIn URL, email/contact route, and 3 personalized support topics.
- Create 3 reusable demo scripts: SaaS, fintech/payments, e-commerce.
- Prepare one short Loom-style demo using a public help center.

Week 2:

- Send 60 highly personalized emails.
- Post 2 LinkedIn demos:
  - "Help center to chatbot in two weeks"
  - "What safe escalation looks like in AI support"
- Build 3 micro-demos for the most promising accounts.

Week 3:

- Follow up with sample questions and screenshots.
- Ask for 15-minute review calls only after a prospect engages with the demo.
- Start agency/implementation partner outreach.

Week 4:

- Convert the best replies into 2-3 paid pilots.
- Publish one anonymous teardown:
  - "We tested 20 help centers. Here are the support questions that should be automated first."
- Refine vertical messaging based on replies.

## Objection handling

"We already use Zendesk/Intercom AI."

Response:

"That may be the right default. The reason teams still talk to us is ownership, custom escalation behavior, EU/private deployment, and workflows that do not fit neatly inside one platform. A small demo will show whether there is a gap."

"We are worried about hallucinations."

Response:

"That is exactly the risk this is designed around. The agent answers from indexed sources, cites what it used, and escalates when confidence is low or the question is outside approved material."

"We do not have time to implement this."

Response:

"The first version only needs your docs URL, handoff route, and a list of topics the bot should not answer. We handle ingestion, tuning, deployment, and handover."

"We can build this internally."

Response:

"If you have the team and priority, that is valid. We are useful when you want a production-ready first version quickly, then source code and deployment config handed over for your team to own."

"What ROI should we expect?"

Response:

"Start with your repetitive ticket volume. If the bot resolves even 20-40% of common questions and improves routing for the rest, the project usually pays back through lower support load, faster first response, and cleaner product feedback."
